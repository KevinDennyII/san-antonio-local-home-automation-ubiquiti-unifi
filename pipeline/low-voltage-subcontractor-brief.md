# Low-Voltage Subcontractor Brief

**Provider** owns design, UniFi config, Home Assistant, client relationship, and QC.  
**Subcontractor** owns structured cabling, AP mounting (as directed), rack dressing to plan, and labeling.

---

## Scope for sub

### Typically included
- Cat6 (or Cat6A) drops from rack to AP locations per Provider AP plan  
- Termination, testing (certification preferred; at minimum continuity + link test)  
- Mount APs / enclosures per Provider model list  
- Patch panels / keystones labeled to Provider scheme  
- Fish existing walls/attics where feasible; notify Provider before cutting finished surfaces beyond plan  

### Typically excluded
- High-voltage electrical (hire electrician)  
- UniFi adoption / SSID config (Provider)  
- Home Assistant (Provider)  
- Drywall finish/paint (unless separately quoted)  
- Alarm panel wiring under alarm license  

---

## How we work together

| Step | Provider | Sub |
|------|----------|-----|
| After survey | Sends AP plan, rack elevation, cable schedule | Confirms feasibility + quote within 3 business days |
| Schedule | Books client window | Commits crew dates |
| On site | Available by phone day of; on site for rack bring-up | Pulls/terminates/mounts |
| QC | Walks links, adopts APs, photographs labels | Fixes punch list within 5 business days |
| Invoice | Pays sub per agreement | Invoices Provider (not client) unless tri-party |

## Labeling standard

```
Rack port / patch: AP-01 LIV-CEIL
Cable sleeve: AP-01
AP sticker: AP-01 Estate
```

Match VLAN docs: Trusted / IoT / Guest SSIDs are Provider’s job after links are green.

- [ ] No visible cable mess in living areas  
- [ ] Exterior penetrations sealed  
- [ ] AP aesthetics approved if HOA / designer sensitive  
- [ ] Test results delivered as PDF or photo log  
- [ ] Work area cleaned daily
