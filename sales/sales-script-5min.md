# 5-Minute Sales Script — Demo Rack

**Audience:** Luxury buyer/seller with realtor present  
**Goal:** Earn a paid site survey — not close a full Package C on the spot  
**Props:** Demo rack, tablet dashboard, one light/plug, rate card + one-pager

---

## 0:00–0:30 — Realtor opens

**Realtor:** “For homes this size, Wi‑Fi and smart devices usually get bolted on after the fact. I work with [Brand/Name] on estate networking and private local home automation — so coverage and control stay with the homeowner.”

*(Hands over to you.)*

---

## 0:30–1:30 — The problem (you)

“A lot of everyday routers — Linksys and similar — have known weak spots in how they’re set up. What you want is a system you actually control, with security first. That’s why we use **Ubiquiti UniFi**.

In a big home, consumer mesh and cloud-tied smart speakers usually also mean dead spots, guests on the same network as locks and cameras, and household data living in someone else’s cloud.

We’re putting **UniFi and local Home Assistant** underneath — a private, closed home-automation system on your network — so the house keeps working even when you want privacy first, or when a cloud has a bad day.”

---

## 1:30–3:00 — Show the network (UDM + APs)

*(Point at UDM Pro and two APs.)*

“This is UniFi — same class of gear hotels and offices use. Dream Machine Pro at the core, wired access points where possible. We carve the home into networks: family, IoT, guests, cameras.

That means a visitor on guest Wi‑Fi isn’t sitting next to your locks. Cameras don’t need to talk to kids’ tablets. You still get one clean Wi‑Fi experience through the house.”

*(Optional: show guest SSID on phone.)*

---

## 3:00–4:15 — Show Home Assistant (tablet)

*(Open dashboard; toggle a light/plug; mention cameras if present.)*

“Home Assistant is the private dashboard for the property — lights, climate, locks, outdoor zones, cameras if we add Protect. It lives on a small computer in *your* rack, not only in an app company’s cloud.

Remote support for us is through an encrypted tunnel — we don’t leave Home Assistant hanging open on the internet.”

*(If Assist works: one voice command — “turn on the demo lamp.”)*

“Voice can stay local with a private speaker and your own wake word — on your network, not someone else’s cloud.”

---

## 4:15–5:00 — Packages + CTA

“We package this three ways: **Network Foundation**, **Private Smart Home**, and a later advanced estate package after the network is stable. Most luxury homes land on Private Smart Home first.

Next step isn’t a huge commitment — it’s a **paid site survey** ($500–$1,500, credited to the install). We map coverage, existing devices, and cable paths, then give you a fixed proposal.

Here’s the rate card. [Realtor] and I can get a survey on the calendar this week.”

*(Leave rate card + one-pager. Confirm who books the survey.)*

---

## Objection handles (30 seconds each)

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| “We already have eero / Google Wi‑Fi.” | “Those are fine for apartments. At this square footage and with cameras/IoT, you want segmentation and wired APs. We replace the weak layer; we don’t rip out every bulb day one.” |
| “What about the voice speakers we already own?” | “Happy to talk through options privately if you want them later. The pilot foundation is UniFi + local Home Assistant + private voice on your network — that stays closed either way.” |
| “Is this DIY / hobby?” | “No — fixed packages, survey, handoff doc, and a monthly Concierge so firmware and Home Assistant don’t become a second job.” |
| “Too expensive.” | “Survey first — Package A alone often solves the Wi‑Fi pain. Many clients phase B after move-in.” |
